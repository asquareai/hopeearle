Karena is a qualified solicitor working in the Family Law team. Practising exclusively in the area of Family law since being admitted to legal practice in June 2021, her experience extends to parenting disputes, Family Violence Intervention Orders, and complex property disputes (including matters with international components). Karena is passionate about attaining just and equitable outcomes for her clients, and empowering clients with tailored advice that meets their practical needs. Prior to commencing employment at Hope Earle in February 2023, Karena worked as a lawyer at a specialist Family Law firm. Before admission to practice, and whilst completing her university studies, she gained practical experience by working part-time as a paralegal in a boutique Family Law firm.

Karena’s academic background is comprised of a Bachelor of Laws (Honours), Bachelor of Psychological Science from La Trobe University, and a Graduate Diploma of Legal Practice from the College of Law. Whilst completing her studies, she explored the issues of family violence and the assessment of families by psychological experts in her Honours thesis. From 2017 until 2018, Karena also worked as a research assistant for a forensic psychology study aimed at identifying risk factors in intimate partner violence. In addition to her experience in the psychological field, her training in cultural competency assists her in engaging with clients as they navigate the Family Law system.

Karena is a member of the Junior Family Lawyers Working Group of the Law Institute of Victoria, Victorian Women Lawyers and the Golden Key International Honour Society.
