Franchising

Hope Earle has an experienced franchising team ready to assist both Franchisors and Franchisees.

Your franchising requirements and your legal documents will vary depending upon your commercial objectives. If you are setting up a franchise system from scratch you will require specific structuring advice and appropriate franchise documents. You will also require comprehensive knowledge of the Franchising Code of Conduct as well as other related legal obligations such as the prohibition against third-line forcing (and the exemptions) and intellectual property rights.

Whether you are a franchisor or franchisee we can assist you with a comprehensive range of business and legal franchising requirements including:


  • Advising on the costs and feasibility of franchising;

  • Advising on your obligations under the Franchising Code of Conduct and Franchise documents;

  • Structuring a Franchise business;

  • Preparing and drafting the necessary suite of Franchise documents for Franchisors, including a Franchise Agreement;

  • Disclosure Statement;

  • Confidentiality Agreement;

  • Lease Agreement;

  • Licence Agreements; and,

  • Negotiating with your prospective Franchisee to get the deal sealed.

As a prospective Franchisee, we are able to help with your legal requirements including:


  • Advising you on your obligations and entitlements under the Franchising Code of Conduct and contracts with the Franchisor;

  • Structuring and incorporating your Franchisee business;

  • Reviewing and advising on the suite of Franchise documents;

  • Employment contracts; and,

  • Negotiating with the Franchisor and/or the Landlord of the proposed franchisee business premises (if applicable) for any desired amendments to the Franchise arrangement.
